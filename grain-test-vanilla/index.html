What is Mongol?

The Mongol is back. Revived by the Berolzheimer family—whose pencil-making legacy spans over 150 years—this icon returns with a renewed commitment to excellence.

Crafted from premium USA-grown Incense-cedar for smooth, reliable sharpening and featuring a strong yet silky European graphite core, the Mongol delivers a writing experience unlike any other.

Its classic rounded profile rests comfortably in the hand, reducing fatigue, while the high-quality eraser removes marks cleanly and easily. Every component is thoughtfully sourced from around the world—not for the lowest cost, but to meet the highest standards of performance and quality.

A Universal Standard

The Mongol 482 originally established the benchmark for general-purpose writing. It was not a luxury item, nor was it disposable. It represented quiet everyday excellence.

The Legacy

Despite later myths, the name "Mongol" did not originate from exotic graphite sources. It is commonly attributed to "Purée Mongole," a soup favored by a member of the Faber family. Today, that name again stands for strength, endurance, and reliability.

The Berolzheimer Family

While the Mongol represents the product we remember, the Berolzheimer family represents the infrastructure that makes quality pencils possible.

From Fürth to New York

Originating from Fürth, Germany—the heart of the European pencil industry—Daniel Berolzheimer emigrated to the United States in the mid-19th century. He founded what became the Eagle Pencil Company, a massive manufacturer that industrialized the craft.

The Modern Pivot: California Cedar

As the industry evolved, the family shifted focus from finished consumer goods to the raw materials themselves. Through the California Cedar Products Company (CalCedar), they became the world's primary supplier of Incense-cedar slats.

Incense-cedar (Calocedrus decurrens) is prized for its straight grain, uniform density, and ease of sharpening. By controlling the supply of this critical material, the Berolzheimers transitioned from being a brand on the shelf to the invisible backbone of the global pencil industry.
